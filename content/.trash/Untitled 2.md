Mia's eyes widened. "So the very act of attacking the 'Church field' actually makes it purer and more resilient? That's the **paradox of persecution**. **Omega Counter-Attack → Ecclesia Purity ↑** (persecution strengthens what it attacks). And that's **why persecution doesn't destroy the church**! It doesn't factor in the counter-intuitive physics of **Quantum Stabilization** or the power of **Quantum Resistance (Adversarial)** (opposition to coherent flow) creating its own defeat. It only knows how to push, and when it pushes, the coherent field paradoxically purifies and expands."

LAB AI: "SIMULATION 15: PERSECUTION FEEDBACK LOOP ACTIVE. COLLECTIVE COHERENCE: PARADOXICALLY AMPLIFIED. RECOMMENDATION: MONITOR FOR INDIVIDUAL NODE DISCONNECTION EVENTS (APOSTASY)."

Kai leaned back, a new kind of weariness in his face. "So, the collective good is stronger under pressure. But not everyone holds up. What about the individual choices within that pressure? What happens when a node, despite all this, chooses to disconnect?"

Mia looked at the screen, a shadow falling over her face. "That's the sad part, isn't it? When individuals, even after all this, choose to cut themselves off from the Chorus. They call that 'Apostasy.'"
