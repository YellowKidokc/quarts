## 1. Project Title  
**Character Limit**: 150  
**Description**: Clearly and concisely convey the essence of your project in non-technical terms.  

---

## 2. Executive Summary  
**Character Limit**: 1,300  
**Description**: Briefly address the following questions:  
- (a) What specific questions will your project answer?  
- (b) What activities will you carry out to answer those questions?  
- (c) Why is this project needed?  
- (d) What concrete deliverables will you produce by the end of the project?  
- (e) What impact will your project have?  

---

## 3. Project Description  
**Character Limit**: 4,000  
**Description**: Provide a specific, concise, and detailed description of the work/activities you will undertake in your project.  

---

## 4. Statement of Significance  
**Character Limit**: 1,300  
**Description**: Describe the current conditions in the field(s) relevant to the project, identify the problems it will address, and articulate the specific opportunity it presents.  

---

## 5. Outputs  
**Character Limit**: 1,300  
**Description**: List the important events and work products (deliverables) your project activities will produce, which are necessary for progress toward your proposed outcomes.  

---

## 6. Outcomes  
**Character Limit**: 1,300  
**Description**: List the specific and identifiable changes you expect your outputs will bring about within 5 years of the project's end date. Describe what success would look like.  

---

## 7. Capacity for Success  
**Character Limit**: 1,300  
**Description**: Explain why your team/organization is positioned to succeed in this project.

## 8. Relation to Sir John Templeton's Donor Intent
