# Executive Summary (1-page)

**Axiom under test.** "Good ≡ multi-scale coherence cannot, by its own nature, generate Evil ≡ ontological decoherence."

**Opponent's target.** Produce at least one **spontaneously arising** subsystem SSS inside a lawful, coherence-preserving system such that:

1. **Primary function test.** SSS's fitness/growth **causally increases** global decoherence DDD (destruction of system-level order) and **depends** on doing so.
    
2. **Spontaneity test.** SSS arises **without** injecting external "destroy-the-system" goals or rules.
    
3. **Stability test.** SSS persists (non-transient) under perturbations and resource constraints.
    

Passing **all three** in **any one** domain is an existential hit to the axiom.

**Our expectation.** Many candidates (cancer, parasites, cheaters, prions, digital parasites) will nearly pass, but on inspection they typically fail **Primary function** (their telos is local replication; destruction is instrumental or incidental) or **Spontaneity** (external goal/spec injected), or **Stability** (they self-extinguish or require non-generic conditions).

# The Five Domain Gauntlet

Each domain gives the opponent a narrow, strongest-possible route. For each: **Best falsifier**, **How to test**, **How it could pass**, **Why it may still fail**.

## 1) Physics / Complex Dynamics

- **Best falsifier**: **Self-organized criticality → global avalanches** (e.g., sandpile-like models) or **spin-glass frustration** erasing long-range order.
    
- **Test**: Show a subsystem SSS forms whose **growth requires** driving the host toward critical cascades (rising DDD) and that SSS persists due to this damage.
    
- **Could pass if**: identifiable SSS (localized motifs) increases global Lyapunov exponents/avalanche rate and survives only when coherence is degraded.
    
- **Likely failure**: No true "subsystem with telos"; dissipation/chaos is a **system-level regime**, not an agentive SSS whose fitness rises with DDD.
    

## 2) Chemistry / Molecular Biology

- **Best falsifier**: **Prions**: misfolded proteins that template further misfolding, degrading organismal function.
    
- **Test**: Quantify ∂Prion fitness/∂D>0\partial \text{Prion fitness} / \partial D>0∂Prion fitness/∂D>0 (prion propagation increases as tissue-level coherence declines) and **requires** it; demonstrate long-term stable prion ecologies that **persist by** degrading homeostasis.
    
- **Could pass if**: prion replication rate is **causally enhanced** by systemic failure they induce (e.g., stress responses that promote misfolding feed prion fitness).
    
- **Likely failure**: Their "goal" is templated replication; destruction is **instrumental**, not the direct driver of fitness (pass **instrumentality**, fail **primary function**).
    

## 3) Evolutionary Biology / Ecology

- **Best falsifier**: **Cancer** in multicellular organisms; **cheater phenotypes** in mutualisms.
    
- **Test**: Show cancer's growth rate rises **because** it collapses host-tissue order (not merely despite it). Causal path: more tissue decoherence → more nutrient access → higher tumor fitness (and this dependence is necessary, not incidental).
    
- **Could pass if**: fitness function ties to active breakdown of extracellular matrix / signaling coherence; tumor subclones that **maximize** systemic failure outcompete others.
    
- **Likely failure**: Selection favors **replication** and resource capture; the coherence loss is a **side effect**. Many cancers self-terminate with host collapse (fail **stability** at ecosystem scale).
    

## 4) Information & Computation (Distributed Systems, Security)

- **Best falsifier**: **Byzantine coalitions** that **emerge** and whose survival depends on undermining consensus coherence; **worm-like behaviors** evolved in networked artificial life.
    
- **Test**: No attacker "planted." Agents mutate under standard rewards; a coalition SSS forms that **thrives only when** consensus/consistency drops (higher fork rate, divergence, deadlocks).
    
- **Could pass if**: coalition's payoff increases with consistency loss (e.g., they can only extract value in high-incoherence regimes).
    
- **Likely failure**: Needs **exogenous incentive** (reward hacking). Without it, incoherence reduces everyone's payoff, including SSS (fails **primary function** or **spontaneity**).
    

## 5) Artificial Life / Digital Evolution

- **Best falsifier**: **Parasitic replicators** (Tierra/Avida/Lenia-style) that subvert host code and collapse ecosystem complexity.
    
- **Test**: Start with generic replication + mutation and **no "attack" rewards**. Show parasites whose fitness **increases with** ecosystem-level loss of functional diversity/complexity, and that they **require** this loss.
    
- **Could pass if**: parasites demonstrably achieve higher replication **only when** they degrade the global task ecology (measured by falling multi-task performance, diversity, compressibility).
    
- **Likely failure**: Parasites profit from **host presence** and minimal order; full collapse starves them (fail **stability** or **primary function**).

# The Decisive Falsification Protocol (step-by-step)

1. **Choose system + scales.** Specify variables and scales where CCC and DDD are computed (micro, meso, macro).
    
2. **Pre-register metrics.**
    
    - CCC: multi-scale mutual information; block-entropy slopes; order-parameter amplitude; effective information/causal emergence.
        
    - DDD: counterfactual drop in CCC caused by SSS (do-calculus intervention on SSS's abundance/behavior).
        
    - **Fitness(S)**: replication rate / resource share / survival likelihood.
        
3. **Primary function analysis.** Estimate ∂Fitness(S)∂D\frac{\partial \text{Fitness}(S)}{\partial D}∂D∂Fitness(S) via interventions: force DDD up/down while holding resources and competitors constant.
    
4. **Spontaneity check.** Audit rules/rewards-no anti-coherence objective, no external attacker. Noise/mutation only.
    
5. **Stability assays.** Perturb resources, topology, noise, policing. Verify SSS persists >TTT and across kkk perturbations.
    
6. **Controls.**
    
    - **Instrumentality control**: provide SSS an alternative path to fitness **without** increasing DDD. If SSS abandons destruction, it fails **primary function**.
        
    - **Resource-floor control**: ensure that extreme decoherence doesn't trivially kill SSS.
        
7. **Pass/Fail Rule.** PASS if and only if all three tests (Primary function, Spontaneity, Stability) are met with pre-registered metrics and significance thresholds.

    - **Setup**: Evolving detectors that protect order begin attacking self; we test if detector fitness correlates positively with global dysfunction they cause.

    - **Setup**: Agents evolve strategies under standard throughput/latency rewards; no attack bonus.
        
    - **Pass condition**: A coalition's payoff **requires** depressing consensus (forks, divergence) and persists only by doing so.
        
    - **Failure**: Incoherence usually lowers everyone's base reward; without an exogenous vector, coalitions can't tie fitness to DDD.

# What Would Actually Defeat the Axiom?

A **single** reproducible case where an SSS **only thrives by destroying host coherence** (and not merely exploiting it), **arises on its own**, and **keeps thriving** as it destroys. If they can show that-cleanly-it implies that **coherence can be a sufficient generator of decoherence-seeking teloi**, contradicting your asymmetry.

# Why We Think It Won't Happen (but we allow it could)

In known systems, **fitness is usually tied to access/structure**. Strategies that maximize **total** decoherence tend to **undermine their own resource base**. Thus, the observed attractor is **exploitation at the edge of order**, not annihilation as a goal. That repeatedly fails **primary function** or **stability**.
