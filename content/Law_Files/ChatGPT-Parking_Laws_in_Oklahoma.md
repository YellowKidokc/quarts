What's an LSTM?

#### ChatGPT:
An LSTM, or Long Short-Term Memory, is a type of recurrent neural network (RNN) architecture used in the field of deep learning. LSTMs are particularly well-suited for tasks that involve sequential data, such as time series prediction, natural language processing, and speech recognition.

Here's a bit more detail:

**Structure:** LSTMs have a special structure that allows them to learn long-term dependencies. Unlike standard RNNs, which suffer from the vanishing gradient problem, LSTMs can maintain information over long sequences.

**Components:** An LSTM unit is composed of a cell, an input gate, an output gate, and a forget gate. These gates regulate the flow of information into and out of the cell.

**Functionality:**

- The **input gate** controls the extent to which new information flows into the cell.
- The **forget gate** decides what information to discard from the cell state.
- The **output gate** determines what part of the cell state to output.

This gating mechanism allows LSTMs to keep track of long-term dependencies and make decisions based on both recent and more distant past information.
