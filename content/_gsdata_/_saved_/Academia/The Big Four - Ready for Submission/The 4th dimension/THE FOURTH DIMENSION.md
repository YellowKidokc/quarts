The Question Everyone's Thinking About

For decades, physicists have quietly wondered: Does human consciousness actually influence quantum systems? It sounds like science fiction, but the data keeps trickling in.

Dean Radin at the Institute of Noetic Sciences recently published results showing 2% deviations in optical diffraction when people focus their attention on the experiment (Radin, 2024). That's small, but with thousands of trials, it's statistically rock-solid. Our preliminary quantum random number generator tests show similar 2.1% biases during deep meditation states.

The thing is, nobody's done this at scale with proper controls. Most studies involve 50-200 trials. We're proposing 10,000+ trials with pre-registered protocols and blinded analysis.

Why This Matters Beyond Physics

Our Approach: Simple Questions, Rigorous Methods

We're testing three specific predictions:

1. Quantum Random Number Generator Bias  
    Can focused human attention shift quantum randomness by measurable amounts? We'll run 10,000 trials comparing meditation states versus control conditions.
    
2. Entanglement Protection  
    Does group meditation preserve quantum entanglement longer than baseline? We'll measure decoherence rates in photon pairs during synchronized contemplative practices.
    
3. Consciousness Field Effects  
    Can we detect "field-like" properties where one person's focused state affects nearby quantum systems? This tests whether consciousness has spatial extent.
